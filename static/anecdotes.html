Client Anecdotes

Anecdote 1: Business Boundaries

Dear Shifu,

I own an essay writing business, where I write essays for students at a 300 Yuan/100 words rate. There is a problem that I cannot be clear about how to solve it. And I need your guidance.

In my business, sometimes certain customer asks me to write an essay for their college applications. After receiving the payment, I write the essay and send it to him. Then he asks if I have time to help him edit another essay he wrote on his own. By edit, it means correct spelling and grammar errors, maybe even content editing, for free.

This is when I feel confused about how to deal with this situation. On one hand, I don’t think I should provide free editing service, because the money they have paid only covers the essay I’ve written for them, and I do provide a paid editing service (100 Yuan/100 words) too. On the other hand, this is an old customer who has used my service several times, so I don’t want to lose the potential future business either.

I see that I have two options here: first, tell him “No, I don’t have time to edit it for free. You can used our paid editing service.”; Second, I highlight the errors in his essay without correcting them, and tell him “ I can check the errors for free because you are an old customer. If you need serious correcting and editing, please choose our paid service”.

Emotionally, I feel more comfortable with the second option, and I’m more used to it. I am more insecure about choosing the first option, so I cannot objectively see the its value to my business and my personal life. I’m not sure how to choose the right option if my goal is to build a healthy business that can support me to be healthy.

My goal for asking your advice is to be clear about how to make this type of decisions, so that I’m being truly responsible for my time and my life.

Thank you for your time. I would really appreciate your help.

Best,
Client


--------------------------------------------

Response

Dear Client,

The answer you are looking for all depends on what is your priority:

  + Do you want this customer’s service?
  + Do you want to have clear boundaries
    with your customers?

You already have your answer, because if you really just wanted this customer’s service you would have accepted their clear disrespect of your boundaries.

Yes, when I say disrespect I mean they are disrespecting you.

They know you are a business (which is proved by their repeated use of your business) and they know what is the boundary here:

  + They must pay for your business

Now they want something for free. That is fundamentally a boundaries issues, and you don’t like it.

You need to take the time (about 1 - 2 hours a day) to sort out the following:

  + What am I so afraid of losing?
  + Am I willing to let my business be
    disrespected to “keep this customer
    happy?”

Only self-understanding, born from time spent in meditation, can clarify this decision for you.

Best regards,
Shifu

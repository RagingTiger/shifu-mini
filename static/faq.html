Frequently Asked Questions

Question 1

What differs between your service and a therapist/psychologist?

Do you want fish, or do you want to learn how to fish?

Do you want emotional comfort, or do you want to learn how to comfort yourself?

Do you want a solution for your problem, or do you want to learn how to solve it yourself?

Our services are solely focused on guiding you through the process of learning how to solve your own problems. This implies our clients are able and motivated to solve their own problems and simply desire some guidance through the process.

Question 3

What does your service consist of exactly?


师父领进门，修行在个人
Teachers open the doors,
you enter by yourself



Our service is simply a dialogue: a conversation between a person outside of the problem and a person inside (the client).

We start by making observations about your problem. We then make suggestions about how you solve this problem. But these suggestions ultimately involve you doing the hard work to face yourself, and solve your own problem. Whether or not you do this work is solely your responsibility.

In the end all we really do is help you see clearly what is your problem and how you can solve it but it is up to you to solve it.

Ref: Question 1, Question 4, and Client Anecdotes

Question 4

Why do you use e-mail?

Writing is the outcoume of rational thought. Unlike voice, text is actually less stimulating and requires more time and patience to craft. It also gives you the convenience of responding when your schedule allows it, and the time to consider your response.

Voice would require you to respond instantly, which in light of the benefits of text, will be of a lower quality response than writing out your response.

Ultimately it is a trade off between lesser quality with more immediacy, or high quality with greater latency.

Question 5

Why can't my problem be immediately solved?

Wanting an immediate solution to your problem and wanting to solve your own problem are mutually exclusive. You must choose one.
